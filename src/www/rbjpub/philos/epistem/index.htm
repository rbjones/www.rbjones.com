Epistemology is the branch of philosophy which studies knowledge and how we come by it. It is therefore a part of philosophy with general methodological significance for philosophy as a whole.

Epistemological problems include:
  • The meaning of words such as "know" (which may not be clearly distinguished from theories of knowledge).
  • Scepticism and its refutation (the justification of claims to knowledge).
  • Broad classifications of knowledge, such as the analytic/synthetic distinction (and their refutation).
  • Particular kinds of knowledge (e.g. knowledge from perception, from memory, or from induction).
