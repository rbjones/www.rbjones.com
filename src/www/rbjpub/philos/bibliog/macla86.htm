Mathematics, Form and Function

by Saunders Mac Lane

Introduction

The questions Mac Lane endeavours to answer come in six groups as follows:
  • What is the Origin of Mathematics?
  • What is the Organisation of Mathematics?
  • Are the formalisms of Mathematics based on or derived from the facts; if not, how are they derived?
  • How does Mathematics develop?
  • Is there an absolute standard of rigour? And what are the correct foundations of Mathematics?
    • Logicism
    • Set Theory
    • Platonism
    • Formalism
    • Intuitionism
    • Empricists
  • Concerning the Philosophy of Mathematics
    • What are the objects of Mathematics and where do they exist (if indeed they do exist)?
    • What is the nature of Mathematical truth?
    • How is it that we can have knowledge of Mathematical truth or Mathematical knowledge?
